# Glossary

Canonical terms for Creative Director OS. Do not invent alternate names when one of these applies.

## Creative Director OS

The system of record and reasoning layer for turning creative intent into authored audiovisual work.

## Studio Brain

The deliberation system that decides which creative problem, uncertainty, proof, or decision should be handled next.

## Living Film Model

The versioned family of typed artifacts and relationships that stores the evolving truth of the authored work.

## Audiovisual Score

The multi-resolution editorial representation above the NLE timeline. It describes viewer experience, craft intent, scene/shot/cut relationships, proof state, and production bindings before execution projection.

## Craft Protocol

A format-specific workflow defining required artifacts, proofs, gates, craft capabilities, review modes, and completion conditions.

## Proof System

The workflow that turns risky creative assumptions into reviewable artifacts before expensive production commitment.

## Execution Layer

The tools and workers that realize approved intent: AI models, NLEs, DCC tools, renderers, humans, media systems, and integrations.

## Review and Learning Layer

The system that records what changed, why it changed, whether the work improved, and which lessons are ratified.

## Script

An input artifact. It may contain narration, dialogue, claims, structure, or intent, but it is not creative source of truth or timeline truth.

## Timeline

An execution projection for media operations over time. It does not replace the Audiovisual Score.

## Audiovisual Event

A designed unit of viewer experience or craft action that preserves Why, What, How, and Where/When.

## Shot Intent

The designed purpose and craft specification for a shot or media event before a candidate asset, capture, or generated take fulfills it.

## Scene Design

The planned event, change, point of view, performance, space, visual strategy, sound behavior, transition, proof, and risk for a scene or designed segment.

## Creative Route

A structurally distinct direction option with thesis, audience promise, story engine, formal concept, production topology, representative sequence, risks, anti-vision, and proposed proof.

## Product Doctrine

The Milestone 0 product philosophy, boundaries, quality model, anti-slop invariants, and refusal logic.

## Anti-Slop Invariant

A hard product rule that prevents default media assembly behavior from replacing authored creative direction.

## Refusal Logic

The documented behavior for blocking, escalating, requesting proof, revising scope, requesting human craft, or accepting explicit compromise instead of producing generic output.

## Decision Gate

A scoped approval point with owner, artifact version, rationale, alternatives, evidence, risks, and downstream consequence.

## Proof

A low-cost artifact or experiment that tests a creative assumption before commitment. Examples include treatment, board, style frame, sound sketch, animatic, edit test, screening, or feasibility check.

## Generic B-Roll

Topic-adjacent media used without specific editorial, evidential, dramatic, spatial, rhythmic, or formal purpose. It is disallowed as silent substitution.
